About David Kolb's Learning Theory

David Kolb's Experiential Learning Theory (ELT) is based on the idea that learning is a process whereby knowledge is created through the transformation of experience. According to Kolb, effective learning occurs when a person progresses through a cycle of four stages.

Concrete Experience (CE)

Learning through direct experience and feeling. This involves being immersed in a new experience and learning through hands-on activities.

Reflective Observation (RO)

Learning through watching and reflecting. This involves observing experiences from different perspectives and reflecting on what happened.

Abstract Conceptualization (AC)

Learning through thinking and analysis. This involves understanding concepts, theories, and ideas through logical analysis.

Active Experimentation (AE)

Learning through doing and testing. This involves actively experimenting with new ideas and testing theories in practice.

The Learning Cycle

Kolb's learning cycle suggests that effective learning occurs when learners move through all four stages. However, individuals tend to have preferences for certain stages, which determines their learning style.

The Four Learning Styles

Based on your preferences in the learning cycle, you'll fall into one of four learning styles:

Accommodator (CE + AE)

Characteristics: Hands-on, intuitive, relies on others for information

Learns best through: Doing, trial and error, group work

Careers: Sales, marketing, management

Diverger (CE + RO)

Characteristics: Imaginative, good at generating ideas, people-oriented

Learns best through: Group discussions, brainstorming, feedback

Careers: Counseling, teaching, arts

Assimilator (AC + RO)

Characteristics: Logical, theoretical, prefers concepts over people

Learns best through: Reading, lectures, research

Careers: Science, research, academia

Converger (AC + AE)

Characteristics: Practical, focused, good at problem-solving

Learns best through: Practical application, simulations, labs

Careers: Engineering, technology, medicine
